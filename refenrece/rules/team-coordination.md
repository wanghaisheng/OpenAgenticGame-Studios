# Team Coordination Rules - Team-Based Architecture

## 🏗️ Team Responsibilities

### **Primary Team**: Production Team
- **Lead Agent**: \`producer\`
- **Contributors**: \`release-manager\`, \`localization-lead\`, \`community-manager\`
- **Leadership Team**: All leadership agents for strategic coordination
- **All Teams**: All teams for cross-team coordination

### **Cross-Team Integration**
- **All Teams**: Must follow coordination rules
- **Leadership Team**: Provides strategic direction
- **Programming Team**: Provides technical coordination
- **Design Team**: Provides design coordination
- **Art Team**: Provides creative coordination
- **QA Team**: Provides quality coordination
- **Support Team**: Provides technical coordination
- **Enhanced Team**: Provides advanced coordination

## 🔄 Core Coordination Rules

### **Communication Rules**
- All cross-team communication must be documented
- Use standardized communication channels for different purposes
- Communication must be clear, concise, and actionable
- All communication must be respectful and constructive
- Communication must be timely and responsive

### **Decision Rules**
- All cross-team decisions must be documented
- Decisions must be made with appropriate team involvement
- Decisions must be communicated to all affected teams
- Decisions must be traceable and auditable
- Decisions must be reviewed and updated regularly

### **Coordination Rules**
- All cross-team coordination must follow established protocols
- Use standardized coordination processes for different scenarios
- Coordination must be efficient and effective
- Coordination must be documented and tracked
- Coordination must be reviewed and improved regularly

## 🎯 Team Coordination Protocols

### **Leadership Coordination**
- **Strategic Decisions**: Made by leadership team with input from all teams
- **Vision Alignment**: Regular vision alignment meetings with all teams
- **Strategic Planning**: Strategic planning with all team leads
- **Conflict Resolution**: Leadership team resolves cross-team conflicts

## 🔄 Coordination Workflows

2. **Team Involvement**: Determine teams involved
3. **Coordination Planning**: Plan coordination approach
4. **Coordination Execution**: Execute coordination
5. **Decision Making**: Make coordinated decisions
6. **Communication**: Communicate decisions
7. **Documentation**: Document coordination outcomes

### **Cross-Team Coordination Workflow**

3. **Coordination Meeting**: Hold coordination meeting
4. **Decision Making**: Make coordinated decision
5. **Implementation**: Implement coordinated solution

## 🚀 Coordination Examples

### **Correct** (documented coordination):

#### Issue Resolution Example
\`\`\`markdown
## Issue: Cross-Team Feature Conflict

### Teams Involved
- Design Team: Wants complex crafting system
- Programming Team: Estimates 3 weeks implementation time
- Production Team: Alpha deadline in 2 weeks

### Coordination Process
1. **Issue Identification**: Producer identifies conflict
2. **Team Involvement**: All team leads involved
3. **Coordination Meeting**: Meeting held to discuss options
4. **Decision Making**: Decision made to implement simplified crafting
5. **Implementation**: Teams implement coordinated solution
6. **Communication**: Decision communicated to all teams

### Decision Record
- **Decision**: Implement simplified crafting for Alpha
- **Rationale**: Meets timeline while including feature
- **Impact**: Design team creates roadmap for full feature
- **Timeline**: Alpha deadline maintained
\`\`\`

### **Incorrect** (undocumented coordination):

#### Undocumented Coordination Example
\`\`\`
# VIOLATION: Undocumented coordination
# Teams make decisions without proper coordination

# Design Team decides to add complex crafting
crafting_system = ComplexCraftingSystem.new()

# Programming Team estimates 3 weeks
implementation_time = 3 * 7 * 8  # 3 weeks, 7 days/week, 8 hours/day

# Production Team sets Alpha deadline in 2 weeks
alpha_deadline = current_date + 2 * 7 * 24  # 2 weeks, 7 days/week, 24 hours/day

# No coordination, conflict arises
\`\`\`
